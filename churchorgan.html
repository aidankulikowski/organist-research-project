Casavant Freres (Canada)

Casavant Frères is a well-known pipe organ building company from Saint-Hyacinthe, Québec, founded in 1879. Over the years, they have built a strong reputation for creating high-quality organs for churches, concert halls, and schools across North America and beyond. Their instruments are respected for combining traditional craftsmanship with modern technology, allowing them to produce organs that are reliable, expressive, and long-lasting. Today, Casavant continues to build and restore organs, keeping their long tradition of excellence alive.

C.B. Fisk (USA)

C.B. Fisk is a respected American pipe organ builder founded in 1961 by Charles B. Fisk in Gloucester, Massachusetts. The company is known for its dedication to hand-crafted workmanship and its focus on creating instruments that closely connect performers with the music they play. Fisk organs often blend historical styles with modern engineering, resulting in instruments that are both artistically unique and mechanically dependable. Today, C.B. Fisk continues to build and restore organs for churches, concert halls, and universities, maintaining its reputation for high-quality, thoughtfully designed instruments.

M.P. Moller (USA)

M.P. Möller was one of the most prominent American pipe organ builders, operating from the late 1800s until the early 1990s in Hagerstown, Maryland. The company became known for producing a huge number of instruments, ranging from small chapel organs to large, complex installations in major churches and public buildings. Möller organs were valued for their reliability, solid construction, and practical designs, which made them popular across the country. Even though the company is no longer active, many Möller organs are still in use today, reflecting the lasting impact the firm had on American organ building.

Aeolian Skinner (USA)

Aeolian-Skinner was a major American pipe organ builder known for shaping the sound of many important churches and concert halls in the mid-20th century. Formed in 1932 through the merger of the Aeolian Company and the Skinner Organ Company, it became famous for its “American Classic” style, which blended elements from different organ traditions to create a versatile and expressive sound. Under the leadership of designers like G. Donald Harrison, Aeolian-Skinner produced some of the most influential instruments of its time. Although the company closed in the 1970s, its organs remain highly respected and continue to be played and preserved across the country.

Cavaille Coll (France)

Cavaillé-Coll was a famous French pipe organ builder from the 19th century, known for revolutionizing organ design and sound. Born in 1811, Aristide Cavaillé-Coll created instruments that were larger, more powerful, and more expressive than most organs of his time. He introduced innovations like new wind systems and expressive stops that allowed for greater dynamic range and orchestral colors, making organs capable of more dramatic and nuanced music. His instruments can be found in many French cathedrals and churches, and his work influenced composers such as César Franck and Charles-Marie Widor. Even today, Cavaillé-Coll’s organs are celebrated for their beauty, richness, and musical expressiveness.

John Paul Buzard (USA)

John-Paul Buzard is an American organ builder known for designing and constructing high-quality mechanical-action (tracker) organs. Based in the Midwest, his company focuses on creating instruments that combine traditional craftsmanship with careful attention to tonal clarity and musical expressiveness. Buzard organs are often built for churches, schools, and recital spaces, emphasizing both reliability and the ability to connect the performer closely with the music. His work is respected for blending historical organ-building principles with modern needs, resulting in instruments that are both beautiful and functional.

Wicks (USA)

Wicks Organ Company is an American pipe organ builder founded in 1906 in Highland, Illinois. The company is known for producing durable, versatile, and affordable organs for churches, schools, and other institutions across the United States. Wicks organs often combine traditional craftsmanship with practical innovations, such as their unique “direct electric” action, which makes the instruments reliable and easier to maintain. Over the years, Wicks has built thousands of organs, and many of their instruments are still in use today, valued for their solid construction and dependable performance.

Allen Organs

Allen Organ Company is an American organ builder founded in 1937 and based in Macungie, Pennsylvania. Unlike traditional pipe organ builders, Allen specializes in digital and electronic organs, using advanced technology to replicate the sound of pipe organs. Their instruments are widely used in churches, schools, and concert halls around the world because they are versatile, reliable, and easier to maintain than large pipe organs. Allen organs are known for high-quality sound, realistic tonal expression, and modern features that allow organists to customize voices and combinations for different styles of music.
