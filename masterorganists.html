Master Organists and Composers

Johann Sebastian Bach 1685-1750

Bach is one of the most famous composers and organists in history. Serving as a church organist in Germany, he wrote many of the greatest works for the organ, including the Toccata and Fugue in D minor. His music is known for its complexity, beauty, and technical mastery, and he greatly influenced organ playing and composition for generations.

Cesar Franck 1822-1890

A Belgian-born French composer and organist, Franck was a master of Romantic organ music. He served as organist at Sainte-Clotilde Basilica in Paris and wrote music that combined rich harmonies with deep spiritual expression. His organ works are still central to the repertoire today.

Olivier Latry 1962-present

A modern French organist, Latry is one of the principal organists at Notre-Dame Cathedral in Paris. Known for his virtuosic playing and improvisations, he performs worldwide and has recorded extensively, bringing both historic and contemporary organ music to audiences today.

Marie-Claire Alain 1926-2013

A celebrated French organist, Alain was known for her interpretations of Bach and other Baroque composers. She recorded the complete organ works of Bach multiple times and was admired for her precise technique and expressive musicality.

Charles Marie Widor 1844-1937

Widor was a French organist, composer, and teacher, best known for his ten organ symphonies, which remain staples of the organ repertoire. He served as the organist at Saint-Sulpice in Paris, one of the most important organ posts in France, for over 60 years. Widor was influential in shaping the French Romantic organ style, combining rich harmonies, orchestral textures, and virtuosic passages. He also taught many students who became prominent organists, passing on his techniques and musical ideals to the next generation.

Cameron Carpenter 1981-present

An innovative American organist, Carpenter is known for his virtuosic technique, modern interpretations, and use of digital organs. He has expanded the organ repertoire by performing transcriptions of orchestral and contemporary works, reaching new audiences for the instrument.
